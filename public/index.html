Things to do

Outdoor Activities

Ladew Topiary Gardens

Beautiful gardens established in the 1930s and located 25 mins from Hunt Valley hotels. They offer a 1.5-mile nature hike or tour of the late 18th century manor house.

More information

Gunpowder Falls Trail

Formerly part of the Northern Central Railway, this relatively flat trail runs for 21 miles along the scenic Gunpowder River and is great for biking, walking or running.

More information

Baltimore Ravens Stadium

Tour M&T Bank Stadium to get a behind-the-scenes look at where the team practices and plays. It is helpful to call ahead for reservations.

More information

Historical Monuments

Fort McHenry National Monument

Located just 10 minutes from Inner Harbor on the Patapsco River, the park is a beautiful place to walk and explore. For history buffs, be sure to tour the fort, which played a crucial role in the defense of Baltimore during the War of 1812. That battle was what inspired Francis Scott Key to pen the poem that became the Star Spangled Banner.

More information

USS Constellation

One of the original six US Navy frigates constructed in 1797 to answer the threat of the Barbary Pirates, the USS Constellation served in Tripoli as well as in the Quasi-War. She is moored in the Inner Harbor and offers daily tours that illustrate sailor life in the early 19th century.

More information

Museums

National Aquarium

See a huge variety of fish species from around the world. The aquarium is located in the Inner Harbor which makes for a great afternoon adventure.

More information

Baltimore Museum of Art

The highlight of a trip here is internationally renowned collections of 19th century impressionists in the Cone Collection, including the largest assembly of works by Henri Matisse. This museum is walking distance from the church for those looking for a little culture before the ceremony.

More information

Food & Drinks

Baltimore has some amazing restaurants. If you're looking to try some genuine Chesapeake crab in the Fells Point, Captain James is not only shaped like a merchant ship, but it serves crabs all year round. Also check out Phillips. They serve award-winning crab cakes and other ocean-fresh seafood.

Here are a few other great spots to grab local fare.
Coffee Shops: Spro Coffee in Hamden near downtown and Afters Cafe in Federal Hill.
Brunch: Woodbury Kitchen in Clipper City, Miss Shirleys Cafe in Inner Harbor, and The Food Market in Hamden.
Dinner: Sotto Sopra, Brewer's Art, Waterfront Kitchen.
